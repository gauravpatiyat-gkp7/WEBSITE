What the badges mean

Four states, read at a glance.

Healthy

More than 90 days of registration left. Nothing to do right now.

Watch

31–90 days left. A good moment to confirm auto-renew is on and the card on file hasn’t expired.

Expiring soon

30 days or fewer — or today. Renew now; some registries start winding the domain down the moment it lapses.

Expired

Past the date. The site can drop offline, and after the grace period the name can be re-registered by anyone.

  • This checks domain registration expiry — not your hosting, DNS or SSL certificate, which lapse on their own timelines.
  • RDAP is the modern replacement for WHOIS. Coverage is broad but not universal; some country-code TLDs don’t publish an expiry date.
  • The date shown is whatever the registry has published, which can trail a very recent renewal by a day or two.
An informational check, not a guarantee. Always confirm renewal status in your own registrar account before relying on it — a domain quietly lapsing is one of the easiest, and most avoidable, ways to lose a live site. — CA Gaurav K Patiyat
