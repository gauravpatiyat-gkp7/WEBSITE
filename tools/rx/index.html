Under the hood

How the numbers work

Net rate (base price)

The MRP on the pack includes GST, so first strip GST to reach the taxable base.

Net rate = MRP ÷ (1 + GST%)
PTR — Price to Retailer

The rate a retailer pays the stockist, after the retailer margin comes off the net rate.

PTR = Net rate × (1 − Retailer margin%)
PTS — Price to Stockist

The rate a stockist pays the company, after the stockist margin comes off the PTR.

PTS = PTR × (1 − Stockist margin%)
Reading a margin back

Given two rates, margin is profit as a share of the higher (selling) price.

Margin% = (Sell − Cost) ÷ Sell × 100

Price control

Are the margins fixed? DPCO & NPPA

India regulates medicine prices through the Drugs (Prices Control) Order, 2013, enforced by the NPPA. Whether you can set your own margins comes down to one thing — is the drug in the National List of Essential Medicines (NLEM)?

Scheduled · NLEM

Price-capped by NPPA

  • NPPA fixes a ceiling price (the maximum MRP), revised each year by the Wholesale Price Index on 1 April.
  • The DPCO fixes the retailer margin at 16% inside the ceiling; the stockist margin isn’t separately capped there (around 8% by trade practice).
  • MRP = ceiling price + GST. Selling above it is an offence under the Essential Commodities Act.
  • Over 900 formulations currently carry a notified ceiling price.
Non-scheduled

Set by the market

  • The company decides the price and the trade margins.
  • Usual norms are 20% to the retailer and 10% to the stockist — this tool’s defaults.
  • The one hard rule: MRP can’t rise more than 10% in any 12 months.

A separate rule (Trade Margin Rationalisation) caps the trade margin at 30% on 42 select anti-cancer drugs. This is a general reference, not legal advice — the NLEM and ceiling prices change, so confirm the current position and any specific ceiling price at nppa.gov.in.

Registration & compliance

Start a pharma stockist business in India

The core steps to set up a licensed wholesale / distribution business.

  1. Register the businessProprietorship, LLP or company — plus GST registration.
  2. Set up the premisesAdequate premises — a minimum of 10–15 sq.m depending on your state (15 if combined with retail) — with storage and a refrigerator for cold-chain drugs.
  3. Appoint a competent personScience graduate with 1 year’s drug experience, or 4 years’ experience without a degree. A registered pharmacist is needed only for retail.
  4. Apply for the wholesale drug licenceForm 20B (general) + 21B (Schedule C/C1), filed as Form 19 on your State Drug Control portal.
  5. Premises inspectionA Drug Inspector verifies the site; the licence is usually issued in about 15–30 working days.
  6. Stay compliantKeep stock, sales, purchase and expiry registers; deal only with licensed parties; pay the retention fee every 5 years.

FAQ

Common questions

What is the difference between PTR and PTS?

PTR (Price to Retailer) is the rate a retailer pays the stockist. PTS (Price to Stockist) is the rate the stockist pays the company or C&F agent. Both are usually quoted as net rates, before GST is added on the invoice.

How do you calculate PTR and PTS from the MRP?

First remove GST from the MRP to get the net rate (MRP divided by 1 plus the GST rate). Take the retailer margin off the net rate to get the PTR, then take the stockist margin off the PTR to get the PTS. The calculator on this page does each step and shows the full breakdown.

Is GST included in PTR and PTS?

Usually no. PTR and PTS are quoted as net rates that exclude GST, and GST is added separately on the invoice. The MRP printed on the pack is the figure that already includes GST, which is why the calculation starts by stripping GST out of the MRP.

What GST rate applies to medicines?

Since the GST 2.0 reform of September 2025, the 12% and 28% slabs were removed. Most medicines are now taxed at 5%, several life-saving drugs are nil-rated, and a few items sit at 18%. Rates are revised from time to time, so set the current rate for your product in the calculator.

What is the difference between margin and markup?

Margin is profit measured as a share of the selling price. Markup is the same profit measured as a share of the cost, so it always looks larger. Pharma rate-lists are quoted on margin, which is what this calculator uses.

Can this calculator work backwards from PTS to MRP?

How do I start a pharma stockist or wholesale business in India?

Register the business and take GST, arrange premises meeting the state minimum (commonly 10 to 15 square metres) with cold storage, appoint a competent person, then apply for a wholesale drug licence (Form 20B and 21B) with your State Drug Control authority. A Drug Inspector inspects the premises before the licence is granted. The full steps are in the registration section above.

What do I need to export medicines from India?

Alongside a drug licence you need an Import Export Code from the DGFT, an RCMC from Pharmexcil, and usually a COPP from the CDSCO, with products sourced from a WHO-GMP certified manufacturer. Each destination country may also require its own product registration.

Are there government schemes or loans for pharma distributors?

Yes. A distributor can register on Udyam for priority-sector loans, use the CGTMSE guarantee for collateral-free credit, or take a MUDRA loan of up to ₹20 lakh. Distributors can also supply the Jan Aushadhi (PMBJP) network of generic-medicine stores.
